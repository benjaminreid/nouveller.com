Question #4 - One thing you wouldn't leave your house without and why?

  • iPhone - i'd go nuts without it
  • My keys. Because otherwise I'd be locked out.
  • My Moleskine notebook for scribbling down the ideas/concepts/plans for supervilliany that bubble up through my consciousness on a daily basis.
  • Mainly my iPhone as it's like having a mini Apple Macintosh in your pocket! Very handy indeed
  • Mobile phone - my link to the design world once I leave the house, and also has tons of images of the main source of my inspiration, my 3 year old daughter.
  • Wallet and phone So I have money and I'm contactable. My phone also has twitter and emails so I almost have everything with me.
  • As boring as it sounds, I'd have to say my MacBook Pro. I'm always running around meeting with clients and showing them things on my computer, it is vital to have it with me.
  • I really want to say my mobile, but I seem to always loose that a lot these days.
    As 'original' as this will sound, I always have a camera on me, whether it be my phone, my instant or even my SLR.
    I've always taken too many photos of my life, I say its because I've got a bad memory so I use photos to remind me of cool stuff that's happened which is kind of true, but really, I just love taking photos.
  • Most probably a sketchbook and pencil so that I can write and draw ideas when they strike me like a thunderbolt from a clear sky.
  • My iPhone, it keeps me connected to everything! But only when I need to be.
  • My iPhone - text, phone, emails and internet access all in one
  • My Nokia E71. I do everything with it. I can access my emails, ring people, text people, take photos, browse the web, store vital notes (that I will always, always forget unless I make such notes) and so much more.

Question #7 - What do you wish you could have designed and why?

  • there's some pretty clever package design out there that i wish i could have designed.
  • The 2012 Olympic logo, as i think it could do with a few 'improvements'
  • http://www.foehn.co.uk/index.asp - Abstract and sexy...even if it does use flash and ASP
  • Anything by Jonathan Barnbrook because he is a cocky sod with big balls. Barnbrook brings issues to light & harnesses the power of design to make change to society. Oh and his typefaces are rather splendid to.
  • Originally, it was the Donnie Darko website which was such a well-designed, abstract Flash based website it inspires to want to design such websites myself.More recently, however, I would have loved to have designed is Google Wave. Such an innovative piece of software, easy to use, and great to look at it in it's simplicity. Roll on the full version.
  • apple.com, simply because its a nice looking website and millions of people use it every day.
  • http://carsonified.com/blog/ - I don't know exactly why, it's just one of those designs I can't get enough of. It's extremely pleasant while not being overdone.
  • Oh man, there are so many. Being mainly a photographer all my life, the design side of things is still quite new to me and I come across things all the time which inspire me and make me sit there and think 'I wish I'd done that'. One which I've been shown recently was http://www.vimeo.com/5437401 video on Vimeo, where they created an advert to promote HP Workstations ability to bring to life anything the creative mind can conceive, and I think you'll agree that it hits the button.
  • Currently, it would be the Mini Ninjas website, its beautifully designed and encapsulates the fun that can be found within the game.
  • A strange one, but some character designs for Wall.e. Because I'd love to be able to draw that well.
  • The initial concepts of the iMac and/or MBP's
  • This is a toughie...I wish I had the creative flair and mind to design some of the pieces of furniture that Marcel Breuer has designed. I think they're really beautiful, such as the Wassily chair, but unfortunately I have no eye for furniture design! I'll definitely stick to my day job.
